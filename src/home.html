ABOUT THE GAME



  • This game is a classic example of Nash Equilibrium.
  • The Nash equilibrium is a decision-making theorem within game theory that states a player can achieve the desired outcome by not deviating from their initial strategy.
  • In the Nash equilibrium, each player's strategy is optimal when considering the decisions of other players.
  • Learn more about this at Nash Equilibrium.

RULES



  • There are 4 teams with a total of 8 rounds.
  • Every team has to catch either 1 or 2 fishes each day.
  • There is a timer of 30 seconds for each team.
  • Profit/Loss of each team will be displayed at the end of every round.
  • The team with the highest profit will be declared as the winner.
